ARCHAE EDITIONS, FarEast BushWick, NY 11385-5751 USA ArchaeEditions@gmail.com ARCHAE EDITIONS is devoted exclusively to the works of a single author, Richard Kostelanetz. Created in response to well-known limitations in the traditional channels of literary communication, ARCHAE (formerly RK), first of all places on sale mint-condition (unless otherwise noted) works of his that are no longer available from their initial publishers--sometimes at remainder prices, other times at prices they would have if published anew today, and, if copies are scarce, at prices in truth essentially for archives. It also collects privately produced editions and from time to time also issues new books, audiotapes, films, videotapes, and holograms. More Portraits from Memory contains visual-verbal portraits so faint they are barely decipherable. Narrative Pictures/Still Lifes is two books whose pages are entwined, each with words and only words realizing the first title continuously on recto pages; only words exemplifying the latter painterly aim continuously on verso pages—in sum, to do with words alone what the visual artist does with pictures. As another example of Richard Kostelanetz’s complimenting English-Centered Writing with Book Art, One-Letter Changes makes narratives out of successions of English words that differ from their predecessors in only one word. One-Line Plays epitomize Richard Kostelanetz’s continuing exploration of extreme minimalism in literature. All these texts are indeed meant to be performed by any numbers of speakers, in a great variety of acoustic spaces. Page-Turners has two-page verbal texts that depend upon the reader’s turning the page from the recto to the verso (and perhaps back again) to be read. Pitches/Turfs/Arenas/Fields (2012) continues Kostelanetz’s geometric poems with words in only the corners of the printed page, the wholes becoming more than the sum of its parts.. Preambles to the New collects in a single thick volume the prefaces that Richard Kostelanetz wrote over the past fifty years. The only writer to do likewise before him was George Bernard Shaw. Repeats Repeats contains richly various English words that, when repeated, assume different meanings. Richard Kostelanetz’s Loves and Lives epitomizes Conceptual Book Art.
