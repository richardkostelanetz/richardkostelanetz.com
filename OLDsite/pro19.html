PROPOSAL for a structurally alternative Autobiographies at Sixty

Like its conceptually similar predecessors, Autobiographies (Mudborn, 1981) and Autobiographies at Fifty (to come), this new book will be not a chronological narrative but a pastiche of autobiographical materials, on the assumption (dear to me) that a gathering contains more truth than any attempt to force a conventional exposition. Nothing from the previous volumes will be included Among the recent essays I hope to reprint are "1962-84," from G. J. Lischka's Alles und Noch Viel Mehr (1985); "Audio Art in America: A Memoir," DLB Yearbook (Detroit: Gale, 1986); the review of Russell Jacoby's The Last Intellectuals, American Book Review (1988); a memoir of applying to the NEH, from The Journal of Unconventional History (1990); "Sephardic Culture and I," from Home Planet News; "Chasing a Chair in the South," "Thoughts on Being Canonized, Sort of," "Discovering Oneself on the Internet," "Editing Book Anthologies" along with a recollection of a particular example; memoirs of Brown and Columbia Universities; and eulogies of George Maciunas and Dick Higgins. One departure will be reprinting Ray Gomez¹s brilliant essay on my fiction. As before, one recurring theme of these essays is surviving as an independent writer. All materials to be typeset can be delivered on discs ready for typesetting. Any publisher interested in considering a preliminary manuscript should contact me. Thank you.
