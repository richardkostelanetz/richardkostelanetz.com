PROPOSAL for a book of essays on Three Canadian Geniuses

It will draw upon essays initially published in periodicals between 1967 and 1990. Most, though not all, have appeared in Richard Kostelanetz books; some were reprinted in anthologies such as William Kilbourn¹s Canada: A Guide to the Peaceable Kingdom (Toronto: Macmillan, 1970) and John McGreevy¹s Glenn Gould Variations (Toronto: Doubleday, 1973). Never before have they appeared between a single set of covers. Here is my best record of the previous appearances of the essays:

"The Glenn Gould Variations," Esquire (Nov., 1967), reprinted in the Kilbourn and McGreevy volumes noted above, as well as my own Master Minds (New York: Macmillan, 1969) and On Innovative Music(ian)s (Limelight, 1989). Review of Otto Friedrich¹s Gould biography, Liberty (1990).

"Understanding McLuhan (In Part),tt New York Times Magazine (Jan 29, 1967), reprinted in several American newspapers (thanks to NY Times syndication) and several textbooks, including Ben B. Seligman¹s Molders of Modern Thought (Chicago: Quadrangle, 1970) and Wilbert F. Moore¹s Technology and Social Change (Chicago: Quadrangle, 1972). It appeared, abridged, as the cover story in Catholic Digest (July, 1967) and in Spanish, Polish, Russian, and Serbian translations, mostly thanks to our USIA. The definitive version appeared in my Master Minds. The second McLuhan essay appeared intially in Twentieth Century (London, Autumn, 1966). It was reprinted in the periodicals Commonweal (USA) and Revista de Occidente (Spain), in addition to several anthologies including Bernard Bergonzits Innovations (London: Macmillan, 1968) and Raymond Rosenthal¹s MeLuhan: Pro & Con (New York: Funk & Wagnalls, 1968). The definitive version appeared in my Twenties in the Sixties (Greenwood-Assembling, 1980).

"The Literature Professors¹ Literature Professor" first appeared in Michigan Quarterly Review (Fall, 1978) and was reprinted in my book The New Poetries and Some Olds (S. Illinois, 1991).

I¹m open to suggestions for making these into a book.
