PROPOSAL for an exhibition of CULTURAL MAGAZINES¹ RETROSPECTIVES

In the course of reading and reviewing for nearly four decades, I¹ve acquired over five hundred examples of a kind of book I¹ve not seen collectively discussed or exhibited before. These are volumes that select from what has previously appeared in a single cultural magazine. Usually they are produced by the magazines¹ editors, especially when the magazine is still being published; sometimes the retrospectives are put together after the magazines has closed. In either case, my term for such anthologies is Cultural Magazines¹ Retrospectives. This collection includes ten books that were issued over four decades by Partisan Review, several from Saturday Review, four from the New Republic, three from transition, several from Mad, two from both Exquisite Corpse and Evergreen Review, one apiece from both runs of the Transatlantic Review, and one apiece from Encounter, Agenda, Smithsonian, Typographica, View, Artforum, Poetry, New Masses, and Psychedelic Review, among other magazines. I also have a few European examples as well--De Stijl, Nouvelle Revue Française. As a veteran contributor to cultural magazines, I assume that a principal reason for collecting such anthologies is that much of the best American writing appears in their pages. Two factors I like about Cultural Journals¹ Retrospectives as a subject for collecting are 1) that no one else known to me is concentrating on them; 2) that the number of appropriate books is finally less than a thousand. One disadvantage is that the category is so unfamiliar I customarily must explain it at least twice, even to booksellers eager to unload their inventories. What Cultural Journals¹ Retrospectives offer intellectually is the opportunity to examine, better than individual issues would, what a magazine has been doing. What I would like to propose is curating a library exhibition devoted to such books, drawing upon my collection as well as whatever other titles might be available in the host library. I would like to write a catalog that, drawing upon earlier writings of mine, would become a classic appreciation of this important, if under-noticed, genre. I assume that this exhibition can tour, its contents changing in detail in each new venue. Interested sponsors are invited to write me at the address at the top of this page. Thank you.
